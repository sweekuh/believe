# Believe — Philosophy Factsheet (Ted Lasso)

Reference for writing the annotation cards. Everything here is sourced. Items I could not verify are marked `[UNCLEAR]`; reasonable-but-unconfirmed inferences are marked `[INFERRED]`.

**How to use this:** each chapter below maps a real philosopher or idea to a moment/character in the show. That mapping is your card pipeline. Card `tag` = the concept, `idea` = the plain-English philosophy, `source` = the citation from here. Always paraphrase the show; never quote dialogue.

## 5. The famous line, handled honestly

**“Be curious, not judgmental.”** Ted writes it on a sign and credits Walt Whitman during the S1E8 darts scene (“The Diamond Dogs”).

The truth: there is **no located source for this line anywhere in Whitman’s writings**, and it is widely treated as a misattribution. <cite index="47-1">Many people were introduced to the phrase through the Apple TV+ series Ted Lasso,</cite> and <cite index="47-1">scholars debate whether Whitman wrote these exact words.</cite> The sentiment fits his work; the sentence does not appear to be his.

Card angle: the show itself models the misattribution, which is a nice meta-moment about how we pass wisdom around. Treat the line as “attributed to Whitman” rather than “by Whitman.”
Source: snugfam.com; fixquotes.com.
